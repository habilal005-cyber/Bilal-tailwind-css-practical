Isaac Newton

1643–1727, Physicist & Mathematician

Formulated the laws of motion and universal gravitation, laying the foundation for classical mechanics.

Developed calculus independently and made significant contributions to optics.

Albert Einstein

1879–1955, Theoretical Physicist

Developed the theory of relativity, fundamentally changing our understanding of space, time, and gravity.

Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for the photoelectric effect.

Marie Curie

1867–1934, Physicist & Chemist

Pioneered research on radioactivity, becoming the first woman to win a Nobel Prize.

Only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different sciences.

Nikola Tesla

1856–1943, Inventor & Electrical Engineer

Developed alternating current (AC) electricity and invented the Tesla coil.

Made numerous contributions to the fields of electricity and magnetism.
